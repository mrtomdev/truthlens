## When to use TruthLens
- You need a **fast triage** as you browse, not a one-shot judgment.
- You want **privacy** — content stays in your browser.
- You're a **journalist, teacher, researcher, parent, or voter** who can't justify a subscription to a closed-source SaaS for ad-hoc checks.
- You want a tool that's **honest about its limitations** rather than selling false certainty.

## When NOT to use TruthLens
- You need a **single official verdict** for a tribunal, lawsuit, or formal academic-misconduct hearing — use a paid forensic service and pair it with a human review.
- You need **bulk API-driven scanning** of large document corpora — TruthLens is a browser tool, not a server.
- You need **document-format scanning** (Word, PDF, image-of-text) — TruthLens scans rendered web content.

## Why on-device matters
When you paste content into a cloud detector, you're handing your text (often someone else's text — a student's, a source's, a confidential draft) to a third party. For sensitive material — pre-publication journalism, legal documents, student work, internal memos — that's an avoidable risk.

TruthLens runs every detector function inside the same browser tab you're already using. No upload. No server log. No vendor incident can leak content that never left your device.

## Migrating from a paid detector
1. Install TruthLens (`chrome://extensions` → Load unpacked).
2. Cancel your other detector subscription.
3. (Optional) Send the cancellation receipt to a journalist friend; this software exists partly to make that conversation possible.

We're not opposed to paid detectors. We *are* opposed to paid detectors being the *only* option for people who need media-literacy tools.
